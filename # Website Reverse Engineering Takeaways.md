# Website Reverse Engineering Takeaways
Source: vashealthandwellness.com
Purpose: Identify strengths, weaknesses, and actionable improvements for our product.

---

# 1. The first 5 seconds must answer three questions
Users immediately ask:
• What is this?
• Is this for me?
• What should I do next?

Design must answer these instantly.

---

# 2. The hero section is the most valuable real estate
Most healthcare websites waste the hero area with generic messaging.

Instead it must contain:
• Clear outcome
• Target audience
• Strong call to action

---

# 3. Avoid generic wellness language
Words like:
• Wellness
• Care
• Health

are too vague.

Users search for **specific problems**, not abstract services.

---

# 4. Messaging should focus on patient problems
High-intent search terms include:

• Back pain
• Knee injury
• Shoulder rehab
• Post surgery recovery
• Sports injury treatment

Content must align with these.

---

# 5. A clinic website must function as a patient acquisition funnel
Bad model:

Visitor → Reads → Leaves

Good model:

Visitor → Identifies problem → Trust → Book appointment

---

# 6. Trust signals must appear immediately
Within the first screen users should see:

• Years of experience
• Certifications
• Number of patients treated
• Reviews
• Insurance accepted

Trust reduces hesitation.

---

# 7. Call-to-action must be dominant and repeated
Primary CTA examples:

• Book Appointment
• Schedule Evaluation
• Call Clinic

CTA should appear:
• hero
• mid page
• footer

---

# 8. The site should clearly define the target patient
Visitors should immediately know if the clinic helps:

• athletes
• workers
• seniors
• post-surgery patients

Specificity improves conversion.

---

# 9. Service pages should be problem-based
Instead of:

• "Services"

Structure pages as:

• Back Pain Treatment
• Knee Rehab
• Shoulder Injury Therapy
• Post Surgery Recovery

This improves both SEO and clarity.

---

# 10. Authority must be visible
Users trust clinicians when they see:

• credentials
• doctor biography
• certifications
• years practicing

This should appear early.

---

# 11. The website should guide the user through a structured journey
Recommended structure:

1 Hero
2 Patient problems
3 Solutions
4 Doctor authority
5 Patient results
6 Booking action

---

# 12. Images should reinforce outcomes
Images should show:

• patients recovering
• therapy sessions
• doctor interaction
• movement improvement

Avoid stock wellness imagery.

---

# 13. Navigation should be minimal
Top navigation should contain:

• Home
• Services
• About
• Conditions
• Book Appointment
• Contact

Too many items reduce clarity.

---

# 14. Mobile design must be prioritized
Most healthcare traffic is mobile.

Requirements:
• fast loading
• thumb-friendly buttons
• short sections
• clear booking action

---

# 15. SEO should target high-intent local searches
Example target keywords:

• physical therapy brooklyn
• sports injury therapy brooklyn
• back pain treatment brooklyn
• knee rehabilitation brooklyn

These bring booking-ready visitors.

---

# 16. The website must act as a conversion engine, not a brochure
Many clinic websites simply describe the business.

High performing sites instead focus on:

• patient problems
• solutions
• trust
• booking action

The goal is to move visitors toward **scheduling an appointment quickly**.
